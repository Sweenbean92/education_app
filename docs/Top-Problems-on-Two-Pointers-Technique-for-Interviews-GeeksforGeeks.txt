The Two Pointer Approach is a powerful and efficient technique used to solve problems involving sorted arrays, searching, and optimization. By utilizing two pointers either moving towards each other or in the same direction, we can reduce the time complexity of many problems from O(n2) to O(n) or O(nlog⁡n). This approach is widely used in competitive programming and real-world applications such as finding pairs with a given sum, merging sorted arrays, and optimizing subarray problems.
